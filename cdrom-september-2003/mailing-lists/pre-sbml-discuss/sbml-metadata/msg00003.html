I do not agree with the proposal of Mike for several reasons:

It took a long time to reach the point of an SBML2 proposal (and we're not
quite there yet, are we?). If this proposal does not include the proposed
extensions, none can see when they'll be included. 

It is very important to include the package and feature stuffs in SBML2, 
which will allow more flexibility in the development of extensions, and in
particular will relieved the stiffness.

Less important:

The proposed changes do not deserve a full number incrementation :-) 

Do you want to keep SBML a California language?

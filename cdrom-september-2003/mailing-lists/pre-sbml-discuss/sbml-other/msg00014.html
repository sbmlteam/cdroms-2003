Folks

Being naive have some questions concerning the
interaction of variables and algebraic rules.

First consider initial conditions:

what happens if the given initial
conditions contradict the algebraic constraints
e.g.
x = 0
y = 1
0 = x + y

2nd consider the actual simulation

looking at it a different way...

should variables e.g. parameters or boundary
condition species that would be constant
in the absence of algebraic equations
vary to satisfy algebraic constraints?

In short do we need an attribute to determine
whether a variable is constant for the duration
of a simulation.  At the moment, algebraic
rules aside, a variable is constant
unless an assignment rule (or reaction in the case of species)
exists for the  variable.  If we put algebraic rules into
the mix I think we have a problem:
what variables are constant and what can vary 
to satisfy constraints?

Have I missed something?
am I being paranoid?   

yours Andrew

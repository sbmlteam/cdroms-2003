Main issues
-----------

(1)

I propose to differentiate between type SId and a new SIdREF, mirroring the
ID/IDREF of XML. There are constraints on id attributes (uniqueness and so
on), which are not the same found for the reference to elements with a
particular id (for instance, existance of a corresponding id). Therefore:

Whatever the element, the attribute id is of type SId, while 

species/compartment
speciesConcentrationRule/species
compartmentVolumeRule/compartment
parameterRule/parameter
modifierSpeciesReference/species
speciesReference/species

are of type SIdREF

(2)

Section 4.8.3: As for Level 1, I am uncomfortable with the output of 
kineticLaw. The expression contained within the math element, as the former
formula, should be in substance/volume/time, and not in substance/time. In
section 3.6.2, it is written "When a specie identifier occurs in a ci
element, it represents the concentration (i.e. substance/volume) of the
species". So  
<kineticLaw>
    <math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/MAth/MathML">
        <apply>
            <times />
            <ci>k</ci>
	    <ci>A<ci>
        </apply>
    </math>
</kineticLaw>

means k * [A], and should represent d[A]/dt

Minor issues
____________

In the second example of section 4.3, the kind of unit is "liter", and not
"liters". 

Section 4.5, the sentence "The optional boolean field [...] of the reaction
system" seems redundant and useless to me. Is it a fossil of former drafts?

In some examples, all numerical values, like initialAmounts and parameters,
equal 0. Are such models very useful? :-)

Regards,

PS: In section 6.1, I did not see any mention of a multistate
enhancement for SBML3. Is the idea totally dropped, or could we keep on
spending time on that issue?

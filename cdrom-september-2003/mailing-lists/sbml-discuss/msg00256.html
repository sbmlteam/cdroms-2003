> As I said, I had only looked at it recently,
> but at the time I wasn't really thinking about
> using SVG in its entirety, just maybe borrowing
> pieces of it. SVG is a spec for painting vector-
> based graphics, whereas what we want is something
> that tells us how to lay out our pathways--far
> less difficult, and much more specific.
> 
> Some of its constructs, however, could be
> useful. For example, how it specifies colors,
> shapes, lines/curves could be used to our
> purposes. For example, I kind of like their
> <defs>...</defs>, something *like* that could
> be useful for defining standard shapes/elements.
> 
> As to my first point, about lots of optional
> detail...my idea is that we allow tools to
> export a fairly detailed description of their
> diagram(s), and, upon importing a model, they
> can simply ignore anything they don't want to
> (or can't) deal with. For example, the Virtual
> Cell will export many, many diagrams, all but
> one of which JDesigner will iggy. When JDesigner
> exports a layout, it might specify that species
> are to be drawn as red rectangles with black
> borders; the Virtual Cell, when it imports that
> layout, will say, "Herbert wanted red rects with
> black borders...bummer...looks like little yellow
> circles it is". This fits with the idea of allow-
> ing a very fine level of detail in the diagrams.
> 
> Best example is the size thing: let's allow a
> size to be specified, and any tool that doesn't
> have a size-based approach can simply iggy it.
> 
> John

> > I have to agree with Kouichi. Concentration is a secondary unit. These should be 
>  > avoided in SBML wherever we can.
> Not possibe! concentrations are what a symbol stands for in the rate law

In level 1. SO let's change the "not possible" in "not straightforward".

> and this is also the way it should be. Otherwise models with 
> different compartment volumes would have to use very awkward rate laws 
> for transport. 

That's the real problem, as nicely exemplified by Andrew a while ago. Is
it really an insurmountable problem?

> Also, usual rate laws express parameters( e.g., Km, Vm, 
        ^^^^^

Could we get rid of this word in the SBML discussions?

1- The "usuality" is a relative issue, depending on one's background and
field.

2- SBML is supposed to be generated and read from tools with user
interfaces. Therefore, the tool's user can still enter concentrations
and volumes, and the SBML printer convert them into amount.

3- It is not because something is usual at the bench that we have to use
it in formal descriptions. It is usual to use bold and italic to give an
emotional side to texts. But (X)HTML uses "strong" and "emphasize"
elements because of many rational reasons. Still, the initial versions of
Netscape Composer changed all the <strong> into <b>. As a result,
<strong>the <strong>current</strong> phrase</strong> became flat.

Biologists also speak with Curies and calories, but we should
use Becquerels and Joules. The incorporation of "usual" units in spatial
programs caused the loss of a Mars probe. The money lost (several
billions $) would have been enough to fund several worldwide vaccine
programs, or fund the research on cancer for several years (or fund for
one percent of the second Iraqi war).

Hi Andrew and Mike,

      Since one of you mentioned that the results of the stoichimetry
question were less than clear, and since I am one of those who cares more
than a little about that, I think it fair to point out an apparent
misinterpretation of the survey results. 

      There were four choices provided as to what to do with
stoichiometry that really boiled down to two.  Whether or not
to convert the field to required MathML.  A total of 19
respondents said no to the MathML requirement (2 said keep
integers only, 8 said change it to double, 9 said change
to either double or a parameter constant) and just 13 said 
Change it to MathML.

      I personally would have voted for a choice not listed:
"Allow MathML expressions in addition to a double or a parameter
constant", but ended up voting for the third non-MathML choice.

      But in the final draft we find MathML (which on the face of it
lost).  And something I did not expect, the loss of any field labeled
"stoichiometry", it is now called "math".  So someone who wants to have
any idea of what this represents has to find it in the document, "math"
gives no hints as to what this is about.  Is that a requirement in making
it use MathML, is that why you did not even present MathML as an option
rather than a requirement, given that the vast majority of models will not
require MathML expressions (or MathML overhead for simple integer, double,
or parameter constant notations)? 

       But do you really think changing this to required MathML and
removing a label that indicates what this field is about is supported
by the survey results?

      I realize this is more substantive than the comments you are
looking for right now, but I do think this really does represent
a survey misinterpretation.  Thanks for considering this.

						Wayne

Dear Mike,

The rate of a reaction occurs several times in the formulas I showed. It 
is only specified once in the SBML <kineticLaw>. Therefore the way it is 
specified must be such that the same formula can be used in all places 
where it is needed (i.e., in all differential equations). Therefore it 
must either be the same formula in all of them, or it must be a common 
part which is extended by the tool. In the examples, in all cases the 
common part for R1 is
k1 A  = k1 [A] Va
which in the cases (CC) and (CA) must be complemented by different 
factors 1/Va and 1/Vb for the equations for dA/dt and dB/dt.
A common rate for an equation can therefore only be interpreted as being 
case (Ax).

The fact that k1 A appars and not k1 [A] would seem to argue for case 
(xA), but (xC) is preferred for other reasons (Michaelis-Menten and 
second order rate laws).

To conclude: (Ax) is necessary for correct interpretation of "rate of a 
reaction", (xC) is preferred due to Michaelis-menten and higher order 
rate laws. Therefore (AC) is used.

Jörg.

  JRW> The reason for choosing (AC) instead of (AA) was
  JRW> probably that enzymatic rates mostly use parameters
  JRW> in terms of concentrations, for which AC would look
  JRW> better. The same goes for higher order reactions. The
  JRW> first order reactions used here are an exception in
  JRW> that the rate constant is the same for formulas (AA)
  JRW> and (CC) if the volumes are all equal. For higher
  JRW> order reactions and Michaelis-menten type rates, this
  JRW> is not the case, and those rates are usually
  JRW> specified in terms of concentrations.

Just a separate note about the distinction between what's
stored in SBML and what a tool shows to the user (this is
probably obvious, but just to keep it in mind): a tool could
very well let users specify rates of reactions in terms of
concentrations and convert that to another form in the SBML.

I don't think you're making this argument, but sometimes
people in the past have argued that things should be in
concentrations because that's how they're usually given.  At
the level of the user interface, that's fine, but what's
actually stored in SBML could be different (within certain
limits, of course, and as long as the forms are one-to-one
convertible).

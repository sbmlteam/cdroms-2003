> To relate HCC to the terms introduced in this book, HCC is actually a 
> *language* (built from a very few primitives) that can be used for 
> modular "multicomponent differential equation systems specifications" 
> (Chapter 7.6, page 169). However, HCC is built on logic and 
> constraints, 
> rather than on "systems theory", so the mappings are not that 
> clear (at 
> least at first shot). A lot of the distinctions introduced in 
> the book 
> do not seem to be relevant in the case of HCC -- for instance,  input 
> and output are symmetric in HCC (both correspond to imposition of 
> constraints); there is no separation between internal and external 
> transitions. HCC is already closed under parallel composition 
> (conjunction), so there is no need for a distinction between 
> "parallel 
> DEVS" and "classical DEVS".  Systems can be "multicomponent" and 
> "networked" -- this is just another way of saying that they 
> are closed 
> under parallel composition (that is, a parallel composition 
> of systems 
> is just another system, hence this operation can be iterated 
> repeatedly 
> to obtain a hierarchically defined system).
> 
> I would be quite interested in whether the theory of this book can

> being built.

I think rounding can easily be accounted for
with a rounded="3" kind of attribute on any
kind of shape that has vertices; the units on
the 3 would be pixels, and it would tell you
how much to round (ie. rounded="0" would be
the default, and indicate no rounding).

We might evenconsider handling all regular
polygons as a single case, like,
<polygon vertices="4" rounded="3" width=...>
would be a square (vertices=4) with corners
rounded 3 pixels. We could also add a
rotated="true" to rotate it theta/2, where
theta is the angle between adjacent vertices;
this could give us upside-down triangles,
for example, or diamonds instead of squares,
etc. Combined with width/height attributes
(which perform scaling), and the prescription
that scaling happens before rotation (or vice-
versa) would give us well-defined shapes.

Or maybe it's just much easier to define triangles,
rectangles, pentagons, hexagons, octagons and
call it good. :)

John

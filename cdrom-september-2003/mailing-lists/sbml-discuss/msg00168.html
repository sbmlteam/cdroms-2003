>> - 'Submodels and Modules' or the story about defined Interfaces.

> if the system is physical, or maybe biochemical, but it is definitely not the
> case for cell biology.

Agreed, but software models reality with structures, often just to be able to
handle the complexity of the model inside the software. 


> (1) Species (and Reactions) are already working as interfaces.   

Reactions? How's that. They refer to substrates, products and modifiers on the
same level and can't be connected to something out of scope.

>       As far as I can imagine, sound practices of SBML can use elements
> inside Reaction
>       or Rule if there are something that should be hidden.  
>        (this might invoke flaming.. but I want to hear your opinions..)
>       If the reaction scheme has some assumptions that hinder changing values
> of intermediate
>       variables (species), then it can be encapsulated in the proposed
> 'reaction mechanism' 
>       element.   (although I don't know the current status of the proposal)

Tell me more about this proposal!
I see problems e.g. with submodels using rapid equilibrium through rules.
If you attach a reaction (and therefore an ODE) to a species that is calculated
by assignment or algebraic rule, you will get a problem with the simulation.
Either the submodel user understands the whole interior or he can use an
interface for the submodel that works robustly.
Not everything is possible to do with this submodel, but the intended use is
reliable enougth.


Anyways: Since I already knew your opinion I want to include both submodels and
modules into the proposal.

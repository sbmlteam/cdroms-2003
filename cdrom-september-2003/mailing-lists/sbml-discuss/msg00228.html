Consider the following: if a biologist knows exact numbers, he/she will 
typically know both the amount and the volume.

When a biologist knows concentrations he/she very rarely knows volumes.

This suggests it is easy to convert from amount to concentration but 
very difficult to convert from concentration to amount.

This would suggest that concentration is more natural.

It has been my experience that except in certain specialized fields, 
such as some areas of pharmacology amounts and volumes are NOT known or 
are very difficult to determine.

I would cast my vote for concentrations as the default measurement, or 
at least having the option to switch defaults from substance to 
concentrations.

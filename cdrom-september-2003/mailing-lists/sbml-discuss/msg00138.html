Following on to my comments yesterday, here are the background references.

There have been papers arguing for the use of hybrid modeling techniques 
for biological systems. The paper "Using hybrid concurrent constraint 
programming to model dynamic biological systems" by Bockmayr and 
Courtois (ICLP 02, Springer LNCS 2401) discusses how discontinuous 
changes may arise when modeling gene expression (Page 3). The need for 
hybrid modeling has also been pointed out by other authors. See e.g. 
references [1] and [17] in this paper.

Vineet Gupta's home page 
 http://www.stratify.com/emp/staff/resteam/vgupta/ The jcc homepage 
(http://www.cse.psu.edu/~saraswat/jcc.html) contains links to the source 
forge code repository, lecture notes on jcc, and some of the HCC papers. 
The jounral paper "Computing with continuous change" describes the 
underlying foundations. The citeseer homepage for this paper is at 
http://citeseer.nj.nec.com/39240.html lists several publications under 
"Hybrid Systems" and "Modeling physical systems" that describe HCC and 
its application to modeling (particulalry in the NASA domain).

In particular, see the IJCAI 95 paper "Modeling time in hybrid systems: 
How fast is instantaneous?" for a discussion of the issue of time in 
hybrid systems. (HCC adopts a simpler approach, dividing the real line 
into points and open intervals, and permitting only finitely many 
"changes" at a time instant.)

Alur et al's CHARON language description can be accessed through 
http://citeseer.nj.nec.com/alur03hierarchical.html  Rajeev et al also 
have a paper on "Modeling and analyzing biomolecular networks" 
http://www.computer.org/cise/cs2002/c1020abs.htm

What is the volume of a compartment with fractal dimension?
 From what I understand about fractals, this is not defined. You can 
define the volume occupied in the space where the fractal is embedded 
(say in d-dimensional space), and this changes as you look at different 
refinement levels (One posible definition of fractal dimension). Or you 
can talk about the extend in one dimension lower (d-1), but this is 
infinite.

Or will you specify volumes as micrometer^2.37 ?
And concentrations as moles/micrometer^2.37 etc ?

Anyway, in nature there are no real fractals, all are multifractals 
(i.e., with different fractal dimension on different space scales), and 
all have ordinary integer dimensions at some limit of small scale. 
Therefore I don't think that fractal dimensions make sense in a 
compartment.

Once we talk about geometrical representation, the idea of fractals as 
generators for complex geometries is vary useful, but with SBML we are 
not there yet.

Jörg.

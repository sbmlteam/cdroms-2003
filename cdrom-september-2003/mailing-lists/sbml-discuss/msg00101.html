Looking at the results and the comments I can't say these results are 
conclusive. With a sample of 16 people, a binomial test shows these results are 
expected 40% of the time if the true split is 50-50. Put another way, the 
observed proportion of 7/16 could indicate a population proportion anywhere 
between ~0.28 and ~0.67 (with 5% confidence intervals). A sample of 16 
participants out of all SBML users (or even all SBML developers, or even the 
Forum participants) hasn't shown agreement significantly different from 50-50. 
The consensus is thus that there is no consensus.

Looking at the detailed comments, we find 4 definite NOs, 3 definite YESs, and 
two which are ambiguous (comments #1 and #8 propose a default "Undefined" 
compartment). It may be that people don't really understand the issue and its 
possible resolutions, so I suggest one of the two following courses:

1: forcibly extract opinions from all Forum participants and treat this as a 
strict majority question of a total population

2: set up an expert task force to decide the question on technical merit

The following paper:

D Eveillard, D Ropers, H de Jong, C Branlant, A Bockmayr "A Multi-site 
constraint programming model of alternative splicing regulation", 
submitted for publication

may be obtained from Alexander's website:

http://www.loria.fr/~bockmayr/

It presents a simple (ODE-based) Michaelis-Menten model for the A3 
splicing site in HIV-1 which can predict splice-efficiency. The results 
of this model correlate qualitatively (at equilibirum) with available 
experimental data.

This model is then used within a larger global model  that describes the 
choice between three acceptor sites A3, A4 and A7. The model captures 
the idea that A4 is the default splicing site, unless the splicing 
efficiency of A3 or A7 rises above that of A4, whence A7 becomes the 
default splicing site. Thus the model uses threshold-based switching of 
differential equations.

The model is expressed in HCC, which is able to utilize incomplete 
information (e.g. interval bounds for unknown parameters) and make 
qualitative predictions.

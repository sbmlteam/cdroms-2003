> Consider the following: if a biologist knows exact numbers, he/she will 
> typically know both the amount and the volume.
> 
> When a biologist knows concentrations he/she very rarely knows volumes.

Do you mean volume should be optional in SBML?

Only concentrations are known is an exceptional case.   
What is exceptional and what is usual is determined not by the number of existing practices,
(I think Nicolas may agree with this...)  but by deducing from the design and structure of
the object model of SBML.   Or, SBML would be caught by DesignByCommittee  or may be
StovepipeSystem anti-pattern.   Another thing we can learn from software engineering :)
(http://c2.com/cgi/wiki?AntiPatternsCatalog)

At the same time, SBML must support such cases where volume is unknown
(as exception).   (being exception does not necessary mean it is not easy to use)

Set the default for the volume be a unit volume, and you get amount == concentration.


-Kouichi

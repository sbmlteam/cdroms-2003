Given that we need a quick resolution to these issues, and given that
there was not a clear consensus on stoichiometries, let me lay out what I
think we need to do and why.

If I understand things correctly, we have different modelers with
different needs, as follows:

a) integer stoichiometries (integer in numerator)
b) rational stoichiometries (integers in numerator and denominator)
c) real stoichiometries (floating point in numerator)
d) a variable name for the stoichiometry, whose value is defined
   elsewhere, whose result could be any of the above
e) an expression for the stoichiometry, whose result could be any of the
   above

Did I miss anything?

Now the point is that different modelers need different things. To express
these different things, at the physical level it looks like a hodge-podge
of fields. But the fact is that, for example, nobody needs a floating
point numerator AND an integer denominator (for example). Am I right?

For the immediate decision, I think its clear that we need to permit the
full flexibility necessary at a physical level to support the various
logical expressions represented by a-c. That means we definately need
floating point numerators and (optional) integer denominators. The only
interesting debate I see is whether we go further and permit IDs or even
expressions. For the immediate future (i.e., deciding by June 1), I don't
care all that much whether we permit IDs and expressions, though I think
an ID would be nice. For the longer term (meaning for a FUTURE release),
its clear to me that many of the problems will resolve themselves once we
have the ability to declare model types or features. In the future, models
should be declared such that its clear whether they have rational
stoichiometries OR floating point stoichiometries, and that it WILL be an
either/or situation for any given model.

On Monday 02 June 2003 18:52, Herbert Sauro wrote:

>
> >- a reaction has a place in the diagram (this place could be the center of
> >the reaction, the place where the arrows meet)
>
> Don't quite understand this, could you explain further. Do you mean where
> the reaction arcs meet? My reaction centers are movable, important if a
> users wants to make nice looking diagrams, hence I store the reaction
> center. By default the reaction centre is the centroid measured relative to
> the participating nodes.
>

This is what I wanted to say. Basically that a reaction has a graphical 
representation and that the minimum set of information that needs to be 
defined is the place where the arcs meet.
 
Actually in our suggestion (http://projects.villa-bosch.de/bcb/sbml/level1/) 
we have two points for a reaction, one where all the substrate arcs meet and 
one where the product arcs meet (we wanted to match the structure of the 
layout to the structure of the model as closely as possible). The two points 
can of course have the same coordinates. 
The two points could also be interpreted as one reaction center and one 
direction (the direction in which the arcs meet at the center).

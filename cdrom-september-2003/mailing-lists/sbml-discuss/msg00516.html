Hi John,

A bit late, but here's my interpretation of the hybrid models discussion
so far (fortunately not a lot has been added to the discussion since
your email, which makes it a bit simpler).

Before the 'official' discussion started, Ed and Vijay agreed that the
Level 2 'hybrid stuff' (events and delays) "simply in isolation from the
full hybrid problem" and "likely to be incompatible with or not
extensible to a full hybrid system".

Ed then listed the things that need to be done, and after some
discussion we settled (well, I *think* it's settled) on the following
tasks and deliverables for the Hybrid Models Working Group:

1. List modelling formalisms.
2. Enumerate 5 sample problems ("challenges") to be addressed by hybrid
approaches.
3. Determine what is needed from SBML to express these models.
4. Determine what, if any, deliverable there is for the Level 3
deadline.

Deliverables:
-  Refinement of terminology and concepts.
-  proposal for SBML L3 extensions (this is not expected to be support
of hybrid systems, but only support of limited capabilities like Events
done with an eye toward future, fuller hybrid systems).
-  challenge problems
-  SBML requirements for Hybrid systems modeling.

The main topic of discussion so far has been what we mean when we say
"hybrid systems" or "hybrid models". Vijay gave a very clear summary of
his view (in
http://www.sbw-sbml.org/sbml-discuss/archive/msg00464.html), with
several refs:

H Matsuno, A Doi, M Nagasaki and S Miyano "Hybrid petri net 
representations of gene regulatory networks", Pacfiic Symposium on 
Biocomputing, 5:333-349, 2000.

R Alur, C Belta, F Ivancic, V Kumar, M Mintz, G J Pappas, H Rubin and J 
Schung "Hybrid modeling and simulation of biomolecual networks" In 
Hyurid Systmes: Computation and Control, HSCC 2001, Rome Italy, pages 
19-32, Springer Verlag LNCS 2034, 2001.

A Bockmayr and A Courtois "Using hybrid concurrent constraint 
programming to model dynamic biological systems", ICLP 2002. (attached:
BockmayrCourtois.ps)

and added in a later email: D Eveillard, D Ropers, H de Jong, C
Branlant, A Bockmayr "A Multi-site constraint programming model of
alternative splicing regulation", submitted for publication (attached:
RappINRIA.ps).

Vijay et al have formalized description of hybrid systems in a language
called Hybrid Concurrent Constraints (HCC), which has been applied to
several biological problems (e.g. by Alexander Bockmayr and coworkers).

Several HMWG members are developing simulators that use continuous
(various ODE) and discrete event (Gillespie/Gibson) algorithms. Kouichi
& Takeshi contributed takahashi03-1.pdf (attached), and Tom sent
Hybrid.pdf (both attached).

Koichi gave his view in
http://www.sbw-sbml.org/sbml-discuss/archive/msg00465.html and he and
Vijay discussed model-simulator dependency (does the simulator determine
the model or should it be the other way round? Vijay says model ->
simulator, Kouichi argues for the opposite, if I have understood it
correctly - it's clearly not trivial. This is an important discussion,
because the shape of SBML Level X will depend on its outcome). See the
rest of that thread for the actual discussion.

In one of my emails
(http://www.sbw-sbml.org/sbml-discuss/archive/msg00477.html), I
mentioned the book "Theory of Modeling and Simulation" 2nd ed., by
Zeigler, Praehofer & Kim. Some, but not all, of the theory in this book
(Discrete Event and Differential Equation System Specification,
DEVS&DESS) has been used for E-Cell by Kouichi (how he used it is in
http://www.sbw-sbml.org/sbml-discuss/archive/msg00485.html). Vijay -
from a 'rivaling' point of view, if I may express it like that - has
commented on the book in
http://www.sbw-sbml.org/sbml-discuss/archive/msg00491.html.

That was it, I believe. Hope it helps.

Cheers,

Maria (now working through the above papers)

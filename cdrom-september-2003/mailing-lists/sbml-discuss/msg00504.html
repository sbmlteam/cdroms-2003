Sorry I didn't answer your question directly.
If you mean encapsulation, yes, anything behind interface is hidden.

Each sub-model specifies what class of simulation procedure to use.
In addition, parameter settings of the engine (Stepper object) can be
specified if it is needed to ensure validity of the model.
For example, stepsize of discrete-time cellular automata is an important
parameter especially in composite simulations, and this should be
given to a DiscreteTimeStepper object.  Although its implementation 
details are encapsulated, the Stepper is a part of the sub-model.

The languages you were talking about may not describe it in this way.
So I think you may now understand what level I was discussing.

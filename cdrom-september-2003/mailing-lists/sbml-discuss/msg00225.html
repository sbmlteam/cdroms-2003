Joerg,

> Remember, SBML is NOT about the internal > representation, but about
the 
> easy exchange of models, and eventually about the reuse of models.

Yes.   And by that reason, amount should be the default.
As Stefan pointed out, it does not make sense to specify volume AND concentration.
(over determined, and can induce inconsistency)


> Not possibe! concentrations are what a symbol stands for in the rate law 
> formula, and this is also the way it should be. Otherwise models with 
> different compartment volumes would have to use very awkward rate laws 
> for transport. Also, usual rate laws express parameters( e.g., Km, Vm, 
> Kc) in terms of concentrations, not numbers.

Of course we are talking about the default.   Concentrations can be used if needed.
It is just one operation to go between amount <-> concentration if there is volume.


> Once you think of composing a model of several submodels, you will find 
> that it is much easier to compose a model from submodels where initial 
> concentrations are specified than from submodels where initial amounts 
> are specified: A submodel that uses concentrations can be included in a 
> composite model with a different volume without change, but a submodel 
> that uses amounts has to be copied and modified, or EACH species has to 
> be overridden by the composed model to get the initial values right.

I don't think this is an issue.   # copies and concentration are
interconvertible.


-Kouichi

> On Thu, 22 May 2003, Wagner,John wrote:
> 
> > First, we HAVE TO HAVE membranes.
> 
> > What we really want is a completely separate
> > element, called membrane; this element will
> > then serve as a container for all of the
> > *other* membrane pieces we want/need
> 
> I strongly disagree with that statement. We HAVE TO HAVE 
> compartments of various dimensionality. The example you give 
> just require a 2-D compartment, sandwiched between 
> 3D-compartments. If we begin to create SBML elements 
> representing biological components, we'll end up having a 
> mapping between SBML and the component vocabulary of Gene Ontology.
> 
> In the 1D, you'll have <microfilament>, <microtubule>, then 
> in addition to the <membrane>, you'll need things like 
> <lipid_rafts> etc.
> 
> <digression>
> There is something to dig here. It would be potentially very 
> useful to have an attribute "goid" attached to compartments 
> and species. <digression>
> 
> And we're just talking about the cell. But Systems Biology is 
> about global study of biological systems, whatever the 
> organizational level. Let's think to the Noble/Hunter 
> simulations of whole organs and individuals ... 
>

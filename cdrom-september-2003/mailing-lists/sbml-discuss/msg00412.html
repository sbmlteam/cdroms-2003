I think I agree in this case with John that the MVC approach is probably a
better alternative having now thought about it a bit more.

However lets not get carried away with what the books say, just because
something is 'generally accepted' as a design principle doesn't mean it must
be used *everywhere*. I read an article recently about why SWING is such a
slow and cumbersome object model (if you've not used any other GUI toolkits
then you'll probably think SWING is the greatest thing ever) and one of the
cited reasons was that the MVC model is blindly used everywhere without any
consideration to other factors, such as ease of use, performance etc. As
someone once said, theoretically pure but practically useless. 

In this case however, it appears that the use of the MVC approach has
greater benefits over the  increased programming effort.

>But again, this is just a generally accepted design principle
>that has been around for a very long time for very good reason,
>and it's so ubiquitous that it has a name (Model-View-Controller)
>and is almost always referred to as MVC. We tend to live by
>this rule (because we're tired of dying by ignoring it).

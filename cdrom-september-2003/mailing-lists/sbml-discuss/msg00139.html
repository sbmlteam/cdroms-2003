Slightly predating XML (and the web, and the rise of open source...) 
here is a link to an effort by several research groups working in the 
area of qualitative reasoning of physical systems to standardize on a 
common modeling language. 
http://ksl-web.stanford.edu/htw/dme/thermal-kb-tour/tour-00.html

The motivation was exactly the same as that for SBML -- there were 
several groups with their own simulators and reasoning tools that worked 
off conceptually very similar but practically quite different notations 
for physical systems. The group more or less worked out a common 
modeling language, but I dont think there was much energy left to 
actually build real tools on this basis. However, the draft is 
available, and there are lessons to be learnt from this effort, 
particularly as SBML moves into compositional modeling (e.g. using 
submodels).

PS: This was done by a group of folks overlapping with the group at 
Stanford (Fikes, Gruber) that went on to lead the ontolingua work that 
Poul cited yesterday.

We would like to remind everyone that the SBML Hackathon is
coming in less than 2 weeks.  We encourage everyone to make
your arrangements as soon as possible.

See the following site for more information:

  http://calvin.bioinformatics.vt.edu/sbml-hackathon

Pedro Mendes's group at VBI is organizing the event, with
sponsorship from Sun Microsystems.

The aim of the 1st Hackathon will be to evaluate and improve
the interoperability of systems biology software through the
use of SBML.  This will involve the live testing of SBML
file interchange of participants' software combined with
software adjustment (hacking) to fix interoperability
defects where possible.  The meeting will involve the
exchange of both Level 1 and Level 2 models; however, we
strongly encourage developers to implement support for Level
2 by this meeting.

Participants are asked to bring to the meeting one or more
models in SBML with humanly readable documentation
describing these models, including simulation output where
appropriate.  During the meeting participants will attempt
to read in the models generated by different groups' tools
and to reproduce the simulation results.  Where
inconsistencies occur between tools, participants will
attempt to fix the problems on the spot.

Registration is free.  Please visit the web site:

  http://calvin.bioinformatics.vt.edu/sbml-hackathon

Mike Hucka, Andrew Finney, Stephan Hoops, Pedro Mendes

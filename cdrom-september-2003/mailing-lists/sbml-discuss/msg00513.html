        Delving into the math to determine whether a modifier is a catalyst (enzyme), inhibitor, or activator is not a very satisfactory answer. Herbert's case of flipping of behavior between activator and inhibitor means that the distinction between these two is blurred, but we should at least be able to tell that from an enzyme whose presence is absolutely required for the reaction to occur.
        I guess transcription factors (repressors, activators) fit into the same mold.

It's been brought up before, but I never saw any resolution:

http://www.sbw-sbml.org/sbml-discuss/archive/msg00155.html

Even if it's best placed in an annotation, it would be nice to have a minimal
set of modifier types defined in the SBML namespace, rather than us all
having to invent our own. E.g.:

...
<modifierSpeciesReference species="S3" modifierType="inhibiting" />
...

OR

...
<modifierSpeciesReference species="S3" >
 <annotation><modifierType value="inhibiting" /></annotation>
</modifierSpeciesReference>
...

I know you're trying to wrap things up, but this seems like a simple and
harmless addition. ; )

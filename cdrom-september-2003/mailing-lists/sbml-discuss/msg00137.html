http://www.w3.org/TR/xml-events/ is a candidate recommendation of the 
W3C for XML events.

This has been used at least by IBM and others for the "X+V" proposal for 
supporting multimodal interaction (markup for combined voice + visual 
interaction). The key requirement for MMI is to support mode switches 
(e.g. from voice to visual and back). XHTML events are used for this 
purpose (among others).

Based on how we resolve the issue of introducing hybrid features, we may 
end up wanting to have a notion of modes for compartments and to switch 
between these modes based on discontinuous changes. We should look at 
XHTML events from this point of view.

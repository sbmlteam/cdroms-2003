Forgive me for coming in late on this. There's been such a blizzard of messages 
and I've had too little time to absorb them all.

I've seen what appear to be two trends in the DWG presentation discussion: one 
focuses primarily on physical aspects (fonts, points, colors, shapes, sizes, 
absolute locations), the other on logical aspects (roles, relative locations, 
styles).

This distinction has been around at least since SGML, and paid at least lip 
service in most follow-on whatever-MLs, and the more favored approach seems to 
be specification in logical style.

For SBML diagram presentation, this would mean a controlled vocabulary for 
functional roles, of which several are available (Cook, Kohn, Tambis, aMAZE, GO, 
BIND, KarpCYC, etc.) Agreeing on a final set is probably an unrealistic demand, 
but agreeing on a minimal set would seem feasible.

Besides, if you sweat out a perfect layout and presentation and it gets reduced 
to an absolute physical description, what happens when your model is composed 
with several others into a larger model? Particularly if each of the component 
models has its own physical layout description?

I'd say: stick with a minimal, relative, logical-type description. The tools can 
render this however they like. Even with yellow circles instead of red 
rectangles. :)

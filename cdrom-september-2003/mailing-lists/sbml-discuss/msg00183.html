Greetings,

The lively 7th SBML Forum meeting held last week in sunny
Ft. Lauderdale, Florida, brought to light a number of
relatively small issues remaining with the SBML Level 2
specification.  The group at the meeting decided not to hold
up issuing Level 2 much longer, preferring instead to set a
short deadline of June 1 for settling the remaining issues
and freezing the definition at that time.  Any issues
discovered after that point will be deferred to an eventual
Version 2 of Level 2 (if necessary), or SBML Level 3.

The remaining Level 2 issues are to be decided via
electronic vote.  The issues are briefly mentioned below:

1) Changing the way stoichiometries are expressed
2) Correct the default units of 'initialAmount' on Species
3) Introduce support for membranes
4) Replace the "Rule" "type" attribute by separate classes
5) Add 'id' and 'name' fields to Events

We will follow a two-step procedure.  First, we will send
out individual explanations of each issue above, so that
people have a chance to think about and debate the topic on
sbml-discuss.  We believe the wider SBML community who
didn't attend the forum in Florida should have the chance to
debate these issues, and we also want to make sure that
everyone gets a chance to point out any additional
considerations, unforeseen implications, etc.  

Second, we will give each topic 48 hours of discussion, then
we will issue URLs for on-line voting.  Our goal is to close
the votes on Tuesday 9am PST 27th May.  This will enable us
to revise the SBML L2 specification document in time for
June 1.

Stay tuned for the detailed explanations of the issues
listed above.

-- Mike & Andrew

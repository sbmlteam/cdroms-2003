On Fri, 23 May 2003, Ed Frank wrote:

> So, I'm wondering if we are coming back to a recurring problem in
> which we want SBML to express any model, but then we fuzz our
> thinking over to a stance in which any model should be interpretable
> by any program and from there to every SBML statement should work
> make sense for any program.  Since the semantic content of the
> models aimed at different simulators can be different, indeed can
> be inconsistent, that just doesn't seem a viable plan.

YES!!!

> Instead, I think the intention of the model needs to somehow be stated
> up front.  A simulator can look at that and decide whether there is a
> match.  Alternatively, using dependency inversion, the *model* can
> state what it needs from a simulator, and the simulator can use that
> to decide if there is a match.

YES!!! This is what I've been arguing for all along. What we want is a
flexible way for modelers to express their model information. But we ALSO
need to let the modelers explicitly express what the model "type" is,
with the consequence that inapropriate simulators will bail out
immediately. This also helps to resolve some of the issues that come up,
such as the peculiarity of a floating numerator and integer denominator.
If you look at as undiferentiated model types with a "one size fits all"
syntax, you probably see this as bizarre and redundanat. But if you look
at it as available fields where the model type further constrains
available usage, its not so bad. Because one "type" of model could require
the numerator and denominator to be integer, and another "type" of model
could require the numerator be integer or float and ignore the denominator.

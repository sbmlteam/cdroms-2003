Understood, and I don't mean to sound like I'm jumping all
over you (I'm not, honestly), but we have to keep in mind
the following:

1) The expressions of reaction rates in SBML are NOT rate laws.

2) Users aren't going to be editing SBML.  What a tool
   presents to the user (could be rate laws) and what is
   stored in SBML, are separate matters.  So just because
   SBML may not use rate laws underneath, it doesn't mean
   the tool can't show rate laws to the user.  (This assumes
   the SBML form is easily transformable from a rate law, of
   course.)

Herbert, your technical expertise would be really welcome in

> > -----------------------------------------------
> > Charter of the SBML Hybrid Models Working Group
> > -----------------------------------------------
> > 
> > Background
> > 
> > Hybrid Models are those that include phenomena modeled in
> > fundamentally different ways or which include elements that change
> > their mode of operation, or both.  For example, discrete and
> > continuous models are fundamentally different.  A model including both
> > would be hybrid as would a system including both non-spatial
> > components (represented by ODEs) and spatial components (PDE's,
> > reaction-diffusion).  
> > 
> > 
> > Mission Statement and Scope
> > 
> > Except for combined continuous/discrete simulation, little hybrid
> > modeling is being pursued in the SBML community [is this correct?].
> > Thus the Hybrid modeling group is at an earlier stage than the other
> > groups and the mission statement reflects this.  Our mission statement
> > is:
> > 
> >    1. Enumerate 5 sample problems ("challenges") to be addressed by hybrid 
> >       approaches.
> >    2. Determine what is needed from SBML to express these models.
> >    3. Determine what, if any, deliverable there is for the Level 3 deadline.
> > 
> > Deliverables:
> >    -  proposal for SBML L3 extensions (this is not expected to be
> >       support of hybrid systems, but only support of limited capabilities
> >       like Events done with an eye toward future, fuller hybrid systems).
> >    -  challenge problems
> >    -  SBML requirements for Hybrid systems modeling.
> > 
> > [This is not meant to be a waterfall process.  I am actually probing
> > here to see if others feel as I do that we are at the very beginning.
> > If so, then some time on seeing if we are all talking about the same
> > things when we say the same words is worthwhile.  The requirements and
> > enumeration of challenges is thus more agreement on a destination than
> > a waterfall methodology.]
> >

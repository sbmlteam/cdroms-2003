On 15 Apr 2003 at 11:08, Eric Minch wrote:

> 1: forcibly extract opinions from all Forum participants and treat this as a 
> strict majority question of a total population

hardly an option, since it is impossible to force people to participate.

> 2: set up an expert task force to decide the question on technical merit

Given that this forum is itself already an expert task force, those who 
bothered responding fit this description well. 

Personally, I vote for NOT allowing default compartments. As someone else 
commented, it is best not to leave anything to interpretation. If models 
without compartments are malformed, then people/software are forced to decide 
what the compartments are like. This forces all programs to also interpret the 
model in the same way. Given that software only has to write a few lines to 
include a compartment (eg a unit volume one, for those models without explicit 
volume components), it sounds to me that this is a no-brainer. Writing is by 
far easier than reading...

Hope my opinion helps sharpening the distribution...

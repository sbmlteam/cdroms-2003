Folks --

I dont understand the rationale behind introducing the restriction 
intended to prevent recursive and mutually recursive definitions.

The MathML lambda element already allows unrestricted lambda abstraction 
and application. That gives you the full power of the lambda calculus. 
In other words,  you already have recursion, cant do anything about it.

(For instance, the Y combinator is just f\(x\f(x x))(x\f(x x)), where i 
have used infix \ for lambda.)

So in any case one has to implement the untyped lambda calculus, thus 
unrestricted functional programming. Why, then, make life complicated 
for the user by not having explicit mutual recursion?

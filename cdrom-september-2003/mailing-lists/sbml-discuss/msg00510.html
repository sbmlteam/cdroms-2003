Charter of the SBML Controlled Vocabularies Working Group
---------------------------------------------------------

BACKGROUND

At the recent SBML Forum meeting in Florida, the Forum
decided to set up working groups to develop proposals for
new features to be incorporated into SBML.  Several people
at the meeting signed up for a working group to develop a
proposal for Controlled Vocabularies (CVs) in SBML.

MISSION STATEMENT AND SCOPE

The mission of this working group is to produce a definition
of extensions to SBML that will allow labeling models and
their components with terms from controlled vocabularies
(CVs).  One of the key objectives of the the CV working
group will be to define a CV describing the features used by
models (the "Feature CV").

This charter doesn't distinguish between the term "CV" and
"ontology".  We expect that some CVs will be hierarchical,
much as ontologies often are, but we leave open the question
of whether the CVs need to be full ontologies.

The group will develop proposals about technologies and
processes to enable continued development of CVs even while
the CVs themselves are in use in SBML.  Also, the CV working
group will develop a series of proposals, each proposal
outlining a single CV.  Each such proposed CV should as much
as possible be independent of SBML.

DETAILS

Each proposal must:

- Describe which SBML elements will be labeled with terms
  from the CV

- Outline the initial CV structure, or else identify an
  existing CV to be used

- Describe how maintenance of the CV will occur, or else
  identify an existing group capable of performing the
  maintenance function.

Main issues to resolve:

- What are the controlled vocabularies that are needed in
  SBML?

- Which SBML elements need to be labeled and how?

- Development of an initial Feature CV.

Termination criterion:

   No CVs will be included into Level 3 until the SBML
   community decides that the existing CVs are mature enough
   for its needs.

   Timeframe: produce an outline Feature CV (together with
   an plan for its maintenance) by the time of the SBML
   Forum meeting at ICSB 2003.

Deliverables:

- One or more CVs for use with SBML L3

- A reference implementation that can read and write the
  proposed standard(s) in a useful software context

- A set of published models labeled according to the
  proposal(s)

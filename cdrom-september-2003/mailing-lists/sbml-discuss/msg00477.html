Hi Ed + others,

I have a book in front of me called "Theory of Modeling and Simulation"
2nd ed., by Zeigler, Praehofer & Kim. I remember listening to a talk on
E-cell (can't remember whether it was given by Takeshi), in which this
book was cited as the 'basis' for E-cell - is that so, Takeshi? It seems
to me that the theory presented in this book is just what we need for
our purposes, but since I'm by no means an expert in this area, there
may be competing theories. Does Vijay have an opinion on this? For the
time being I'll assume that Zeigler et al. have done a good analysis of
the process of modelling & simulation, and use what I've learned from
the book so far.

To get back to your email, Ed, Zeigler et al. distinguish 3 types
(formalisms) of dynamic model specification (as opposed to e.g. object
modelling, in which there is no concept of time): the Discrete Time
(DTSS), Differential Equation (DESS), and Discrete Event (DEVS) System
Specifications. DESS includes ODE and PDE descriptions. DTSS includes
things such as cellular automata (which are updated as a whole in a unit
'time' step). I think (but I'm not entirely sure) that DEVS includes
stochastic models (in which possible state transitions have certain
probabilities), but also threshold models (in which a state transition
occurs when a certain parameter crosses a given threshold). Have I
understood this correctly?

> Would the following modifications to the charter incorporate 
> your comments fully:
>   1. elaborate "discrete" in the example text

I don't think you need to elaborate discrete (in the introduction) any
further, as you're using it in a very general way, but maybe you could
leave out the "Except for combined continuous/discrete simulation" bit,
because even that sounds too grand in the light of what people are
currently doing (to my knowledge). 

>   2. Add before "enumerate 5 models," "enumerate simulation 
> types/modes/frameworks"

Yes, I think that would be a good idea (btw, when you say 'enumerate',
do you mean 'list', or does enumerate have any further significance
(like: we need to give each one a number)?). Maybe "list modelling
formalisms" expresses more precisely what we're going to talk about than
"simulation types/modes/frameworks"?

>   3. Add as a deliverable, refinment of terminology/concepts.

Agreed. Very necessary.

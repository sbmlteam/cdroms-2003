On Wednesday, May 7, 2003, at 09:52 Pacific/Auckland, Herbert Sauro wrote:
As I said I'm not one way or the other, I just cautious. We are getting
MathML in Level 2 anyway. I just want to make sure that we're ready to give
the standard the required software support (which hopefully will come).

Does anyone know of any other XML based standards that use MathML as a means
to store mathematical expressions (except CellML)? It would be interesting
to see how they worked out in practice.
Here are a few examples that may be of interest:

MathML (both presentation and content) can be generated from Mathematica using the ExpressionToMathML command in the XML`MathML` context. See http://documents.wolfram.com/v4/AddOns/XMLCapabilities/GeneratingMathML.html for details.

WebEQ from Design Science http://www.mathtype.com/en/products/webeq/ handles both content and presentation MathML. I believe that it uses MathML as its native equation representation.

Amaya and Mozilla from W3C can display both content and presentation MathML. The underlying presentation engines are flexible enough to embed SVG in MathML and vice versa.

The IBM techexplorer Hypermedia Browser provides full support for MathML 1.01, both presentation tags and content tags.

Mathcad 2001 from MathSoft uses content MathML as a format for serializing its internal representation of mathematical expressions.

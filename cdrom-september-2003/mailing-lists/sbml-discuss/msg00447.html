In theory, it is SAFEST to express molecular species in moles (i.e. as
numbers). HOWEVER, second order rate constants and equilibrium
dissociation constants and Michaelis constants are normally expressed in
units of concentration (and time). In an equation such as the much used
S/(K + S) (S = species, K = eq diss const), K and S must both be
concentrations because of the way K is defined. Similarly, in dC/dt =
k.A.B, where k is a 2nd order rate constant, at least one of the species
A and B must be a concentration, and if the rate is a concentration over
time, both must be concentrations. 
Therefore, in practice it is SENSIBLE to use concentration units by
default, particularly in SBML L2, since it it built, as it were, on
enzyme kinetics conventions. The fact that you can have intialAmount or
initialConcentration confuses the issue, but as long as it is spelled
out clearly and makes people happy, it may not matter too much. Tools
that deal with transport between compartments, or in which there is
another source of possible confusion, should be very rigorous in dealing
with units, and communicate potential pitfalls very clearly to their
users. But this is not the job of SBML.

In SBML L3 this whole discussion will move to the parameter sets set,
won't it?

Maria

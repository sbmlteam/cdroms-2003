It became pretty clear to me in Florida that people
wanted the ability to specify diagrams in great detail
and with great flexibility. The general consensus
was, I believe, that diagrams must allow for multiple
"views" (ie. multiple diagrams), and tons of optional
attributes (which tools are allowed to ignore to the
extent necessary).
For example, in the Virtual Cell, we have one
"compartmental diagram" which lays out species
and compartments, as well as a diagram for
each compartment (where all the species and
reactions in that compartment are laid out)
and a diagram for each membrane (which
includes the membrane, the compartments on
either side of the membrane, and all the species
and reactions in those compartments/membranes).
On the other hand, all we keep track of really
is species locations (we draw all the reaction
details ourselves, basically, and use our own
symbols for species, reactions, etc). Thus, we
would ignore any species diagram info except
for (x, y). But, when writing out an SBML file,
we'd put in the markup describing our species
glyphs (eg. yellow circles with black borders
1 pixel thick, black text for species labels,
all the coordinates for the reaction arrows,
etc); thus, when you brought it into Jarnac,
it would be laid out identically (in a perfect
world, anyway) in Jarnac as it was in VCell.
 
Damn it's late. 4 AM. I hope that made sense.

	Andrew,
	
	You are correct - the proposal does focus on a schematic
	or circuit diagram layout application.  What struck me
	was that the tags defined in the proposal provide much of
	what one would need for a visualization of a spatial simulation
	during runtime or as a post-simulation playback.  The addition
	of a third dimension to the spec would help for these types of
	application (plus the shape, color or colour :-) 
	There are some other attributes of an object we might consider, but
	the list could grow indefinitely, particularly if we try to support
	molecular geometries/conformations.
	
	One point sprung to mind when I read the schematic proposal: the
	coordinates are all using 2D cartesian coordinates with an implicit
	origin.  Do folks use polar/spherical coordinates at all (potentially
	a help for molecular representations)?
	
	Greg

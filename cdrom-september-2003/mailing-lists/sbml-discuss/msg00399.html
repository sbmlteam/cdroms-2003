> This only works as long as you only have a substance once in a 
> reaction. Imagine the same substance would used as substrate and 
> product in the same reaction. This would mean we would have to use 
> another attribute to denote the function. Would work as well, but as 
> soon as someone gets the idea to specify a reaction like 2*A -> B as 
> A+A -> B, it wouldn't work anymore!

That is one of the motivations for separating
the "view" from the "model".

In the language of my prototype
http://caboy.uchc.edu/wagner/Science/VirtualCell/SBML-DWG/FutileCycle-0.xml
we'd have something like,

<sbml ...> ...
<view ...> ...
<listOfDiagrams>
	<diagram name="DiagramName">
		<listOfSpeciesGlyphs>
			<speciesGlyph species="A" x="127" y="241" />
			<speciesGlyph species="A" x="912" y="711" />
			<speciesGlyph species="B" x="238" y="564" />
		</listOfSpeciesGlyphs>
		...
	</diagram>
	...
</listOfDiagrams>
</view>
</sbml>

This makes it clearer and easier to specify what the species
is (in the model) versus how the species is used (in the view).
Putting the diagram spec in with the model spec means that
whatever constraints you have on the species in terms of the
model are de facto constraints on the diagram, which is clearly
undesirable.

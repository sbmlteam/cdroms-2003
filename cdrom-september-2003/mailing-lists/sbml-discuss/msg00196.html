Ah, sorry; I should have explained that better.

It's not so much being able to express 2.335/123, but
rather, the idea of being able to express a stoichiometry
either as 

- a (single) floating-point number
- a fraction (integer/integer)

If you take out denominator, you can't express fractions,
which was the whole reason that stoichiometry ended up
having the form integer/integer rather than a single
floating-point number in SBML Level 1.

It's true that having a floating-point 'stoichiometry' field
and an integer 'denominator' field leaves open the
possibility of someone using both simultaneously, leading to
bizarre 1.23/567 numbers.  I guess we'd have to specify that
this shouldn't be done.  (And yes, having do that wouldn't
be very elegant.)

My personal opioion (I have not discussed this with anyone)
is that only a completely open standard will work.
We already have people from companies involved who are using SBML
I think they would be disappointed if SBML or part of SBML 
had a commercial license.  Such a license would be
a serious impediment to the wide adoption of SBML
as a standard.  I would like to encourage the
adoption of SBML by corporate entities and view the marketing
of software which imported and/or exported as indication of
our success.

That said it makes sense for SBML, given demand, to include a generic
mechanism
for the linking of models to diagrams.  This might allow a model
to be linked to a representation of a proprietory diagramatic notation
such as GNS's DCL.  Such a representation would be encoded in a
proprietary
xml format.

In short given the demand we should investigate ways to
link models to diagrams in proprietary formats.
However we should not include into SBML 
features that restrict SBML's use by any party.

There was another issue raised at the meeting, which I only
poorly understood (as usual) and so can only vaguely
describe here.  Hopefully someone else can chime in and
expand this more correctly:

The point was that a floating point number for a
stoichiometry may not have the same interpretation in a
stochastic regime as the same number expressed as a
fraction.  The reason is that converting the float to the
fraction may result in a number like 2300/4700 or whatever,
and on the face of it, a tool reading such a stoichiometry
might interpret this as a statement about the numbers of
molecules involved in a reaction, and this may have a
different meaning than than a fraction like 0.4894.

Someone else can hopefully reply with a better explanation
about this point, but anyway, this issue was brought up...

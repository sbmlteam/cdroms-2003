Hi John,

I didn't intend to raise a new discussion on this but..

> S0, and S0 catalyzes E? Then you would have
>  
> S0 --E--> S0*   with rate Kcat1*E*[S0]/(Km1 + [S0])
> E --S0--> E*    with rate Kcat2*S0*[E]/(Km2 + [E])
>  
> That is, you'd need different dimensions depending on use.

I don't think this is problematic, as long as we have an explicit way to indicate which
are in conc. and which are in amount.  (such as Conc( S0 ), or [S0] or S0.Concentration,
or whatever)

Vcell is going beyond enzyme kinetics.   You may agree with us that this flexibility to have
both conc. and amount is very helpful in cell biology modeling.


> But, in the end, you aren't getting around the problem, because
> if your volume changes, you'd *STILL* have to scale Km.

Something must be changed, depending on the pivot.   
I know you have to change it because you want to preserve the reaction rate and the
substances.    And the other case is to preserve rate constants and substances, allowing
changes of the reaction rates.

It seems this discussion is recurring.   I'll stop here.


> As I said, there is no way around this, so we're going to
> have to pick the lesser of two evils.

Agreed.

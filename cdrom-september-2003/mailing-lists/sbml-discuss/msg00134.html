The way they did it was to split the project into the three terms we had,
first term lexical, second term syntax, third term code. If I remember right
they had to develop it using Algol 60. 

I think part of the reason why they were able to do this was because one of
the young profs called P J Brown had written the book Writing Interactive
Compilers and Interpreters, it's a nice book, very readable, which describes
how to write a BASIC interpreter for then new fangled microcomputers (1979).
There was a lot of emphasis on efficency, trying to pack an interpreter in
to about 8 K of RAM etc. Those were the days....

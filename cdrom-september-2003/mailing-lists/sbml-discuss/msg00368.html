Andrew,
        Obviously done in a hurry (there are no lowerboundYs and duplicate upperboundX s and many of the coordinates don't make sense).
        Do we need a center rather than co-ordinates for top left and botom right of a rectangle that we can fit the required shape or annotation box on (and co-ordinates for each end of straight lines - with a middle for curves)? Center is a mean value of the bounds which is so simple to calculate that it seems pointless to specify.
        Also, do we need negative integers in the co-ordinates?
        We could argue about whether we define 0,0 as top left (some screen drawing packages) or bottom left (as in paper), but most should agree with whatever we decide on. It's no great hassle to change something like that to draw upside down if you've already chosen the other option.
        We'd not need units here, though text will inevitably get messy from time to time (-thanks Herbert for your reassurance). Then the 1/72nd inch default could go into the stylesheet too, which puts it where most of the arguments have been all the time.
        This would let us have a core which could be agreed quickly, and the stylesheet CV arguments (including fonts, shapes, colours, mapping of sizes to co-ordinates, and little yellow circles) could be allowed to rumble on without affecting the basic standard.

Folks

Just some clarification of the stylesheet idea:

a) I'm suggesting a split between on the one hand
                i) the model and minimal diagrammatic information
and on the other
                ii) detailed rendering information

(i) is as logical as possible but isn't purely logical
i.e. includes just enough information to render the diagram
without most unintended crossing artifacts.  It would
exclude color, bitmaps, fonts, shapes etc.

(ii) is described in stylesheet form

b) a stylesheet can be included inside the model document
or be external or be overridden by the user i.e. you don't
need a separate document necessarily.  Anyway the minimal
diagrammatic information should be enough to do a basic rendering
of the diagram without a stylesheet.

Concretely a model file would consist of:
a) the Level 2 stuff as now but labeled using a CV like that given by
Eric (thanks)
b) a series of minimal diagramed views of the model
c) each dioramic view would have an associated stylesheet
                which may or may not be in the document

here's a very crude example, forgive any weird chemistry please

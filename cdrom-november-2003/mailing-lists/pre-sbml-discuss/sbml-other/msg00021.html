Cliff

Many thanks for your contribution to the development of SBML L2.

I think it would be useful, at least for me,
if you could post to sbml-other some examples of 
Tyson's models in the rough structural form that
you would like to transmit in SBML together with,
if possible, the published papers that are associated
with those models.

These examples don't have to be in XML
some form of structured ASCII would be fine.
XPP files would be fine although I don't think
that's what you're thinking of.

If you do this we may be able to discuss
the encodings of specific models
and thus perhaps make some concrete progress
and/or get a better feel for where the
specific mismatch is if it exists at all.

My specie issue is this:

I have not seen in a Tyson's models
(nor for that matter "hand-crafted" models from anyone)
equations which abstract math over all the 
reactants or products of a reaction i.e. the rate equations
reference specific reactants and products.

Hence I'd like to know about models
where these abstractions are an important
requirement.

(We have seen a requirement for this kind
of abstraction for machine constructed models
where the effective structure of the model
is learnt using machine learning.)

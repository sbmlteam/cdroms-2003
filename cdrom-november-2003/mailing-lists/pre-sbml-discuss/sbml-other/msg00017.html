Hi All,

     Cellerator allows you to define Reactions with a shorthand arrow
notation which are then expanded to appropriate DiffyQs (see
http://www-aig.jpl.nasa.gov/public/mls/cellerator).

     When we read in our own Reactions, we would like to map from the
Reactions with its Reactants, Products, KineticLaw, and Parameters
back to the shorthand arrow notation.

     As far as I can tell, there are two ways to do this:

       1.  Annotate each reaction with a type, something like:

             <annotation>
               <cellerator:reaction type="Conversion"/>
             </annotation>

           and similarly for each Parameter.

       2.  Employ a graph matching algorithm to do the same in the absence
           of such annotations.  This is an (NP) hard problem and a unique
           solution is not guaranteed.

     Obviously (1) is easier, but (2) is preferable since it may allow us
to better read and understand models from other simulators and my group
(Machine Learning) has significant experience with such algorithms.

     Why would we want to read our own models?  We are completing a first
round-trip from Cellerator to and from our internal pathway modeling
database.  This trip can involve SBML, but is currently lossy.

     We will probably pursue (1) for the time being, but I wanted to
run this by the group / community to see if anyone had suggestions or
comments.  Are we missing something obvious?  Are we insane?

     Thanks.

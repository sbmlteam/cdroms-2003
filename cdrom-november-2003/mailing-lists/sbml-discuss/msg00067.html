Preliminary announcement: 
The 7th Workshop on Software Platforms for Systems Biology
----------------------------------------------------------

Dear all, 

We are planning on holding the next SBML Forum on May 11-12.
As discussed in Stockholm, we plan on co-locating this particular
SBML Forum meeting with the BioSPICE PI meeting (May 13-16), so
that members of the BioSPICE Model Definition Language subgroup
can more easily attend.  The BioSPICE group has narrowed its
possible locations to two: Chicago or Pasadena, California.
We will let everyone know as soon as we find out the final
choice of the location.

Please let us know if you would like to attend the meeting
or if this time or location(s) pose a serious problem.
[We can provide limited financial support for travel expenses
where necessary.]

At the moment we have the following provisional agenda items:

- Final agreement on the form of SBML Level 2

- Introductions to SBML parser libraries from Virginia Tech and
  Caltech

- Proposals for model composition for SBML Level 3
  by Martin Ginkel and Jonathan Webb (??)

- Discussion of the form of parameter sets

- Discussion of other features for Level 3 depending on advance
      demand including stochastic representations, diagram support,
      complexes and spatial representations.

- An introduction to the BioPAX pathway standard initiative
  by Gary Bader (see http://www.biopax.org)

- Plans for the formal organization of SBML Development

- Plans for a submission of a proposal based on SBML to the
  OMG in response to LSR RFP-14.  For information, seen see
  http://cgi.omg.org/docs/lifesci/02-10-01.pdf (note that
  the LOI deadline has been changed to July 28)

  We hope that some key members of the OMG LSR will be able
  to attend to answer our questions.

  The idea behind connecting to OMG is to create legitimacy
  for SBML through an existing standards organization and to
  ensure that any OMG standards in this field meet the needs
  of the SBML community.  However, there are potential
  implications of this that the entire community needs to
  discuss.

- Short presentations announcing new developments in the community
	
Documents from the 6th Workshop are available at:
http://strc.herts.ac.uk/bio/stockholmweb/stockholmforumhomepage.htm
and the 5th Workshop at:
http://strc.herts.ac.uk/bio/hatfieldforumweb/hatfieldforumagendapapers.h
tm
http://strc.herts.ac.uk/bio/hatfieldforumweb/hatfieldforumhomepage.htm

We plan to post to this mailing list a revised draft of the SBML
Level 2 document and a paper on parameter sets before the end of
March.  We believe Martin Ginkel will post a proposal on
model composition before May.

We encourage discussion on these agenda items on the sbml-discuss
list in advance of the meeting.  This will make the meeting
potentially more productive.  Please let us know
any additional agenda items you think are missing or if you
wish to make a short presentation on a new development.

Best regards,

Andrew Finney & Mike Hucka

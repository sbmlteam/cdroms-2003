Hi Kouichi,

On 21-May-2003 Kouichi Takahashi wrote:
>> If you attach a reaction (and therefore an ODE) to a species that is calculated
>> by assignment or algebraic rule, 
> 
> Probably I couldn't get your point, but implicit solvers can handle such 
> an situation? And a species connected to only rules or assignments has a 
> limited ulitity?

For assigmentrules in pure ODE: No.
(Either the integrator assigns the state depending on the derivative or the
assignmentrule does it, not both)

For algebraic rules in DAE: It depends. If the resulting system has index>1 or 
becomes contradictory it will not work. My idea is, that the modeler of the 
submodel will take care of these difficulties and will expose only `robust' 
elements...

> It is not necessary a case for E-Cell 3, since it uses asynchronous 
> discrete event approach, but I understand the problem.  

Can you solve Index 2/3 DAE? I know this may work with some integrators but
I don't know good stepsize adoption for these beasts. (We didn't find a robust
solution yet).
I should have a look into the details of the new ECell, especially for 
the asynchronous coupling of different algorithms.

I am afraid that I am more radical: I want to suggest an alternative
to using a subset of SVG or building anything custom to render full
details about the diagram (e.g., shapes, color, fonts) in an attempt
to design the third layer that Sven Sahle defined.

The alternative can be: not to embbed the rendering in SBML, but
instead to link to fully rendered diagrams (such as described in
my previous post:
http://www.sbw-sbml.org/sbml-discuss/archive/msg00245.html).

I believe that uses for high-quality, fully rendered diagrams
are not the same as for diagrams that let visualize the reactions
in a model, and interact with the species to decide what concentration
should be plotted.

In my opinion, these two use scenario do not overlap enough to
justify mixing these two aspects in SBML.

If you think differently pit would help if you could describe
use scenarios for fully rendered diagrams in the  context of
simulation/modeling.

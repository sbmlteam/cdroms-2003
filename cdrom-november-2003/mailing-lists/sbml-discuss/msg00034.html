On 19 Dec 2002 at 17:00, Andrew Finney wrote:

> However we should not include into SBML 
> features that restrict SBML's use by any party.

I totally agree. We do not use anything in COPASI that carries restrictions on 
its distribution or forcing us to do anything with the code. Basically we use 
only libraries which allow distribution of their runtime, anyhting like Apache 
license (which is IMHO the best) or public domain. We will certainly NOT use 
any language that is protected by a patent, even if the licensors promise to 
keep it free of restrictions. They can always go bust, and the patent ending in 
someone else's hands who is not so kind (e.g. the infamous case of the .gif 
unisys patent).

I also doubt that those patents that are being mentioned in PS of previous 
posts would hold in court. There is just too much prior art. (a good example of 
this is Taq polymerase, which got invalidated in court).

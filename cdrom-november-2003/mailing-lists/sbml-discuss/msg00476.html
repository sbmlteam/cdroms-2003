Hi Maria,

Thank you for the note.  In answer to your question, "yes," i.e.,
"yes, I am not sure which discrete I meant" AND, "yes, _both._" It is
all part and parcel of enumerating simulation types and clarifying
terminology.

Would the following modifications to the charter incorporate your
comments fully:
  1. elaborate "discrete" in the example text
  2. Add before "enumerate 5 models," "enumerate simulation types/modes/
     frameworks"
  3. Add as a deliverable, refinment of terminology/concepts.

Where I am coming from: I am trained as a high energy particle
physicist and have worked with, but not written, large simulations,
e.g., simulations with millions of model elements and simulations with
large run times (10's of CPU years 8 years ago.  Since then they have
probably racked up a multiple of 1000 CPU years).  I have written
simple simulators for particle interactions which I suppose you would
call discrete time (but not time on a grid). I have handled random
number management over large run-sets and data management. I have
worked on application frameworks for this kind of processing,
frameworks supporting executables constructed from 100 to 1000
computational modules in a code base of about 4 million lines of C++.
In that context, I've worked on release management, parameter
management and, in general, pulling out great quantities of hair.  A
year ago I started into computational biology at Argonne National Lab
where our interests are in whole-cell modeling with an eye towards
engineering organisms for hydrogen production and carbon sequestration
(greenhouse gasses).

	-Ed

ps. Although this thread is outside the SBW discuss mailing list, I'd
like to see us collect this thread and post it to the list once we've
agreed on the proto-type charter.  Would that be ok?  Then people will
see the history and join in on refining the charter.

> > > -----------------------------------------------
> > > Charter of the SBML Hybrid Models Working Group
> > > -----------------------------------------------
> > > 
> > > Background
> > > 
> > > Hybrid Models are those that include phenomena modeled in 
> > > fundamentally different ways or which include elements that change 
> > > their mode of operation, or both.  For example, discrete and 
> > > continuous models are fundamentally different.  A model 
> > including both 
> > > would be hybrid as would a system including both non-spatial 
> > > components (represented by ODEs) and spatial components (PDE's, 
> > > reaction-diffusion).
> > > 
> > > 
> > > Mission Statement and Scope
> > > 
> > > Except for combined continuous/discrete simulation, little hybrid 
> > > modeling is being pursued in the SBML community [is this correct?]. 
> > > Thus the Hybrid modeling group is at an earlier stage than 
> > the other 
> > > groups and the mission statement reflects this.  Our 
> > mission statement
> > > is:
> > > 
> > >    1. Enumerate 5 sample problems ("challenges") to be 
> > addressed by hybrid 
> > >       approaches.
> > >    2. Determine what is needed from SBML to express these models.
> > >    3. Determine what, if any, deliverable there is for the Level 3 
> > > deadline.
> > > 
> > > Deliverables:
> > >    -  proposal for SBML L3 extensions (this is not expected to be
> > >       support of hybrid systems, but only support of 
> > limited capabilities
> > >       like Events done with an eye toward future, fuller 
> > hybrid systems).
> > >    -  challenge problems
> > >    -  SBML requirements for Hybrid systems modeling.
> > > 
> > > [This is not meant to be a waterfall process.  I am 
> > actually probing 
> > > here to see if others feel as I do that we are at the very 
> > beginning. 
> > > If so, then some time on seeing if we are all talking about 
> > the same 
> > > things when we say the same words is worthwhile.  The 
> > requirements and 
> > > enumeration of challenges is thus more agreement on a 
> > destination than 
> > > a waterfall methodology.]

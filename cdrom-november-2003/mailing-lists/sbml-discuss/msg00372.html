	Wagner,John wrote:
	> Oops. In my original email, I forgot to point out
	> that charge is an optional attribute, and that is why
	> it seems like it *must* have a default.
	> 
	
	Actually, not specifying a charge is not the same as accepting the
	default. Therefore it does make sense that the optional field charge
	does not have a default, or in other words, the default is "undefined".
	
	
	If charge has a default, then tools that use the charge (BTW, are there
	any yet?) would make a mistake if they read a model without charges and
	assumed that all species have charge 0. It is more likely that the model
	creator did not care to collect that information, or that the dependence
	of charge on PH-level could not be coded in SBML.

  shaffer> I think there's another argument beyond the fact
  shaffer> that having to put the same number in two places
  shaffer> is error-prone. By making these two places be the
  shaffer> same variable, you are expressing an intention
  shaffer> that they in fact have some relationship. This is
  shaffer> pretty much the same reason why we were adamant
  shaffer> about permitting defined rate laws. Sure, we can
  shaffer> get the necessary mathematics at some physical
  shaffer> layer by repeating the same mathematical
  shaffer> expressions in multiple places. But when you give
  shaffer> a name to a concept, and then use that name in
  shaffer> multiple places, you are making explicit the
  shaffer> relationships in your model. That might let your
  shaffer> software do more.

Yes, you're right, that's a very good argument.  I think
someone had voiced it at the meeting too, but I forgot to
mention it in the message.  Thanks for bringing it up.

MH

On 22-May-2003 Kouichi Takahashi wrote:
> I haven't decide my stance on this issue, but at least
> I agree it can be useful to have rational and fractional stoichiometries because
> I got some requests to support them from users.

I think the main point having doubles there, is the use of software that works 
only on stoichometry numbers but wants to be aware of phenomenons as biomass 
production (this is a model simplification where the empiric parameter is recorded 
just at the wrong(don't beat me) place). 
In our models the same parameter (produced biomass per drained precursors) is instead
used in something that I can only express as a rule in SBML.

Does it make sense to assume that a model from a e.g. flux analysis tool will directly be
used in a dynamic simulator without changes? Is this an important use-case?

Since I don't really assume identical round trip transition of models 
(toolA -> toolB -> toolA and toolA finds exactly the same model)
between model editing tools, can't this be solved by the tool's importer?
E.G. the Ecell importer doesn't want to have double stoichiometry and just 
sets it to 1 and adds another rule/fast reaction to put the value of prduced biomass 
right?

I think we will have a problem with 1:1 mapping of SBML -> Tool-Model in many cases.
As we already found in some inofficial talk: SBML fits very well to Jarnac and Gepasi ;-)

My Opinion to stoichiometric coefficients: use one double number and no denominator
and record an issue to `Best Practice' that preferred are numbers with frac(x)=0.0.

I was under the impression that we'd be getting rid of the
denominator field altogether. What's the utility in expressing
a stoichiometric coefficient as 2.36435/12345? (That's not a
sarcastic remark, I'm wondering if there might be some kind
of use for it among, say, the stochastic folks, etc, that I'm not
seeing).

	  JWagner> going a bit far--with no payback whatsoever
	  JWagner> outside of the issue of having the same constant
	  JWagner> in two places being error prone (I'd point out
	  JWagner> that having the same MathML expression in two
	  JWagner> places is equally, or, arguable, a LOT more,
	  JWagner> error prone). Moreover, this "simplification"
	  JWagner> means a big complication for developers, who
	  JWagner> would now be forced to evaluate these MathML
	  JWagner> expressions in their stoichiometry code. As
	  JWagner> always, it's a matter of priorities, and we have
	  JWagner> much bigger fish to fry (for example, I'd MUCH
	  JWagner> rather allow initial conditions to be MathML
	  JWagner> expressions--far more useful, with far more
	  JWagner> payoff).
	
	OK, so if you're ok with allowing for floating point numbers
	in the 'stoichiometry' field, what is your feeling about the
	'denominator' field?  Should that remain an integer?

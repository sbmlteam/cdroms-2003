On Tue, Oct 14, 2003 at 11:57:40AM +0100, Andrew Finney wrote:

> Are you suggesting that we label modifiers with somekind of controlled
> vocabulary to indicate the modifiers role in a reaction?


I continue to believe that what is mandatory or not depends upon the
intention of the model and the meaning of the model elements. Also,
this intention should not be left to inference by the application
because it is hard enough that we will all do it differently.  It
should be stated as part of the model + modeling language.  Given this
information, An application can decide if it can satisfy the
intentions/needs of the model.

This is the exact opposite of the current approach, as I understand
it, which is to make the language such that models will satisfy some
sort of union over the requirements of the present pool of
applications and the meaning or intention of the model is carried
in the understanding of the sbml documents and correct sniffing by the
application.

I'm trying to decide if the present discussion is an illustration of
this.  I think it is. It sounds like the disagreement in the discussion
originates in different applications needing reaction specifications
to mean different things (and that need is unresolvable and fundamental).

The only way out I see is to specify in the model what the _model_
means and let apps decide if they can satisfy the needs.  "This is a
flux model."  "This is a stochastic model."  "This is an ODE model
with an explicit list of rates."  "This rx is really an annotation (eye
candy) on the following rate equation"  "This rx defines the entities
from which to generate equations."  etc.

To wear it on my sleeve: I believe that models, fundamentally, are
_not_ portable across all applications. The game is to record what the
model means and facilitate the transfer of those parts that can be
transfered.

Folks

Chris Cox at the University of Tennessee has built a stochastic
simulator
that supports "simple" kinetics and Hill kinetics.  He is in the process
of using SBML L2 as part of DARPA BioSPICE.  He is at the stage of
reading and 
writing his own SBML.  Interoperability is next.  (Chris discussed
this at the last MDL conference call)

There have been requests for controlled vocabularies for models - to
help
software decide whether they can process a model - and for reactions
- many software packages don't process free form rate laws but
instead use a controlled vocabulary instead.

We intend to have this on the agenda in Florida.

> The trouble is we only currently have stochastic mass action 
> laws, note that a stochastic rate law has to be dervied for 
> each mechanism, and as far as I know we only currently have 
> mass action law in stochastic form. I think Chris Rao has 
> done some work in trying to derive stochastic Michaelis 
> Menten laws but I don't know how successful he was. I have 
> seen at least one accepted paper (it was rejected by three 
> referees) which made an inexcusable error in a stochastic 
> model, they tried to use a hill equation as a stochastic law 
> with out any justification. I think it's safer to let the 
> computer derive the rate laws otherwise users are likely to 
> enter all sorts of laws which violate one or more laws of 
> physics. If we want to allow users complete freedom in their 
> choice of rate laws then the simulator must be able to 
> identify any rough rate laws.

Dear SBMLers,

The Tokyo and Caltech teams have put together a first SBML
Collection CD-ROM, to be distributed at the ERATO Final
Symposium next week.  You can browse the contents online
here, courtesy of Akira Funahashi:

  http://sbserv.symbio.jst.go.jp/~funa/cd-rom/

We did this under significant time pressures and did not
have the time to include all of the things we had originally
wanted (and that were discussed at the SBML Hackathon), such
as a search facility and more software.

Using this CD-ROM as the starting point, we are now planning
a *second* CD-ROM in time for ICSB 2003.  We hope to
distribute the CDs as handouts to all attendees at the
conference.

At this time, we would like your feedback about what you
like and don't like with the current CD (see URL above), as
well as suggestions for additions.

We would especially like to add more software tools to the
CD.  The software may be entirely open source, or it may be
demos of closed-source commercial offerings.  However, note
that we can only accept the latter as long as there are no
restrictions on distributing the demo version or its
installer on the CD or with a web version of the CD that we
will put up on sbml.org.

Oct. 10 is our deadline for getting all the materials.
Please submit your additions to sbml-team@caltech.edu.

Those of you who are working on new releases of your
software may wish to prepare the release in time for
Oct. 10, so that the latest and greatest version can be put
on the SBML Collection CD Release 2.

Mike [for sbml-team@caltech.edu]

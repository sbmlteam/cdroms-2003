John, you gave me less than 10 hours to reply before posting
this.  On some days, that's not enough time for me.

Speaking just for myself, I hate web-based forums, for the
following reasons:

1) You have to remember to go to a specific web site and
   check if new activity has occurred.  While this is not so
   much a problem if the forums have been active and you
   remember to do it every day, when there are lulls in
   discussions, it's much too easy to forget to check.

2) I can't archive the discussions for myself (or at least,
   most web-based forum systems don't provide a way to
   download all the messages).  It's very important for me
   to keep discussions messages together by topic, in my own
   archive, so that I can search, copy, etc., easily.  It's
   much, much, much slower to go through a web forum system
   than to keep my own archive of messages.

3) (The most important issue for me:) You have to use your
   web browser's editing interface.  This is incredibly
   inefficient.  It's something like 100 times more
   efficient for me to use my normal mail editing interface
   (emacs) than typing into a text box.  Typing into an
   editor and then copying+pasting into a text box is not a
   good solution to this problem.

Of course, I recognize that just because *I* have a total
aversion to web-based forums, it doesn't mean other people
agree.  So here I'm just stating an opinion, and if other
people want to use this approach, that's ok too, and I'll go
along with it.

I do see that you did a very nice job creating the forums,
complete with graphics!  I don't mean to belittle your
efforts.  I think you did a very nice job.

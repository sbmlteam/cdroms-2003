hugh.d.spence@gsk.com wrote:
> 
>         Delving into the math to determine whether a modifier is a 
> catalyst (enzyme), inhibitor, or activator is not a very satisfactory 
> answer. Herbert's case of flipping of behavior between activator and 
> inhibitor means that the distinction between these two is blurred, but 
> we should at least be able to tell that from an enzyme whose presence is 
> absolutely required for the reaction to occur.
>         I guess transcription factors (repressors, activators) fit into 
> the same mold.
> 
Can someone remind me where to find "Herbert's case of flipping of behavior 
between activator and inhibitor"? I can't seem to find it, and I'm working on 
something related, so would really like to see additional examples.

In any case, it seems premature to include such functional roles as tags within 
the model. They are actually fairly high-level constructs, and I'd argue that 
they belong more in the presentation specification than in the model specification.

Furthermore, wherever they are eventually to be included, it would seem prudent 
to enumerate them as part of the Controlled Vocabulary effort rather than as ad 
hoc fixes in the model.

Stefan

I totally agree with your views: SBML != Biospice.

I'd like to clarify a few issues:

The most important issue with respect to SBML standardization
is concensus.  We won't incorperate anything into SBML unless
its clear a very significant majority of the community, that
is people reading this mailing list and attending forum
meetings, think the given proposal is acceptable. That is why
I wrote "If there is concensus..." in my original posting
(see below).

Strictly within the bounds of the above principle it's important
that we communicate proposals and stimulate discussion of those
proposals; and then once we have a concensus that we incorporate
them into the standard. If we don't do this SBML will become
irrelevant: an irrelevant standard is not any use.

In the case of Jonathan's proposal I was under the impression
that it incorporated enough features from previous discussion
on sbml-discuss, including the EML proposal, so that it could
be used as a basis for a concensus and thus something we could
incorporate. It is obvious that I was wrong about that.

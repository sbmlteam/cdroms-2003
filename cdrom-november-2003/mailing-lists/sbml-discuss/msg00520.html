Off the top of my head:

The rate law:

k1*X - k2*X^2 

flips behaviour.

The species X behaves as an activator at low concentrations and an inhibitor
at high concentrations. This rate law has been used in the past to model
positive feedback.

pH of course is a classic example (hydrogen ion) of an effector where enzyme
activity flips from activator to inhibitor.

Other examples include the effect of bivalent ions and temperature.

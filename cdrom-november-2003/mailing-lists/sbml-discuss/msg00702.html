Hi Herbert,

     I'm not sure what you mean by "guidance".  Can you elaborate?

     Just to clarify, libsbml places no restrictions on what species
can and cannot be modifiers, i.e. in any reaction, reactant and product
species may also be listed as modifiers at the discretion of the model
builder.

     The only guidance libsbml offers is a function that "computes"
modifiers based on a reaction's rate law and then adds them to the
reaction.  The algorithm is simple: for each symbol in the KineticLaw,
add the corresponding modifier(SpeciesReference) iff: i) the symbol
represents a species in the current model, and ii) the symbol does not
represent a reactant or product in the current reaction (see previous
emails to sbml-discuss and biospice-mdl for more information).  If
nothing else, this function allows one to be compliant with the SBML L2
specification with no effort beyond what is required to specify a
reaction in SBML L1.

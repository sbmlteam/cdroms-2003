On Mon, 26 May 2003, Wagner,John wrote:

> The 1/72 is a "point", and having been used throughout the
> publishing world since the beginning of all time, it is a
> very common unit. I suggested it because it seemed less
> arbitrary than, say, 1/96 or 1/1,294,856,912,560,117 or
> something. :) Also, it isn't a function of the metric or
> English system, it's simply a definition of what a point
> means. That said, it's not exactly well defined. In some
> parts of the world, 1 point = 0.351 mm, whereas in other
> parts of the world, 1 point = 0.376 mm.

It is even a bit more complex than that. 0.351 mm is actually a "point",
but it is not 1/72 in, it is 1/72.27 in. 1/72 in is the "Big point" or
"Postcript point". 0.376 mm is the "Didôt point", that is 1/72 french
inch.

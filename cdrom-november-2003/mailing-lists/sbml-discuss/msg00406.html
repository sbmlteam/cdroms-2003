Sven Sahle wrote:-
- a species can have several graphical representations.

        You'd have a job producing a complex diagram if this weren't true. Ubiquitous molecules like ATP occur many times (with identical representations) in a pathway. Proteins can be substrates and products of reactions as well as inhibitors and enzymes.

- a reaction has a place in the diagram (this place could be the center of
the reaction, the place where the arrows meet)
        As Herbert said, not necessarily at the center, but the intersection of the arrows.

- the graphical representation of a speciesreference is a line or curve in
the diagram leading from a reaction center to a graphical representation
of a species.
       
        I think of the reaction as the sum of the center and the arrows. The speciesreference is merely an instance of the species. As the arrow have directionality (which is an important part of the reaction) and speciesreferences don't, mapping one on top of the other seems like a bad idea to me.

Yes indeed, I forgot about rounded squares, that should be included. As for
the residues sites I'm not sure what to do about that, I saw your document
on this and it's look good, in fact I should look at it again as I've
probably missed out a couple of shapes. The residue sites brings up a number
of issues which we should consider, how do we represent complex composite
shapes, eg a gene/promoter figures, or Hiroaki's residue sites?

Maybe we'll have to reconsider SVG after all, unless we can devise some
means of specifying composite shapes built from our basic shapes which I'm
sure we could. With composite sites another issues comes up, where should
reaction/interaction arrows end up when they approach a node? Shold they
point to the center of a node, or some particular aspect of a node shape, eg
a residue site on the node, or the promoter site on a gene shape? Lots of
issues to consider! 

Herbert

> > As to my first point, about lots of optional
> > detail...my idea is that we allow tools to
> > export a fairly detailed description of their
> > diagram(s), and, upon importing a model, they

> > (or can't) deal with. For example, the Virtual
> > Cell will export many, many diagrams, all but
> > one of which JDesigner will iggy. When JDesigner
> > exports a layout, it might specify that species
> > are to be drawn as red rectangles with black
> > borders; the Virtual Cell, when it imports that
> > layout, will say, "Herbert wanted red rects with
> > black borders...bummer...looks like little yellow
> > circles it is". This fits with the idea of allow-
> > ing a very fine level of detail in the diagrams.
> > 
> > Best example is the size thing: let's allow a

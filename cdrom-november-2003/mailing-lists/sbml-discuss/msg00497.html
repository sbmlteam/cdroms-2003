Folks

Does anyone have objection to the following modification to
the species structure in SBML Level 2?

'units' attribute is replaced by 'substanceUnits' and 'spatialSizeUnits'
attributes.

the units of 'initialAmount' is determined by 'substanceUnits' only

the units of all other uses of the species is
determined by 'substanceUnits'/'spatialSizeUnits'
(i.e. concentration)

The uses of these concentration units are:

a) the units of 'initialConcentration'
b) the units of species symbols in formulae
c) the result units of assignment rules
d) concentration/time forms the units of rate rules

In fact this 'concentration' will be know as 'the 
units of the species'

[
 Why do we this change?: because 
	a) initialAmount and initialConcentration have different but
related
		units.
	b) we need to be able to determine exact units for each species
		separately (rather than say deriving it from the
compartment units.

 Why can't you break out the substance component from a composed
	concentration unit definition? : because
	the unit scaling can't be reliably associated with
	just one part of the composed unit

	e.g. molesx10^-6/litre === moles/litrex10^6
] 

Any comments very gratefully received.

Hello all, 
 
Hugh Spence wrote: 
>If a substance is both a substrate and a product, you could cancel it out 
>as not taking part, or split the reaction into it's actual component 
>parts. 
>i.e  a+b>C C+d>e+a could have been joined to give a+b+d>e+a. 
>        The two reaction process is more accurate and wouldn't cause 
>problems. 
>        The one reaction process could be written b+d>e. In this case no 
>data is lost by eliminating the a from the reaction (if you put it in as 
a 
>modifier to show that it must be there). The process of combining the 
>reactions compromises the model, not the elimination from both sides. 
> 
>The 2A->B is a case where the person doing the splitting is creating his 
>own problem. We have enough real problems without solving problems the 
>users should have avoided for themselves? How they represent it inside 
>their own software is their business. If it can be done in SBML, how 
their 
>internal representations map to it is the users' problems. Certainly we 
>should advise them that the alternate representation could have this 
>problem. 
 
I think we should base our diagrams on what models are allowed in SBML and 
not on what model we think are good models. While I do agree that 
generally 
the reaction 2*A -> B should have 1 speciesreference with multiplicity 2 
it 
is valid SBML to have to speciesreferences. So this 2 speciesreferences 
are 
identical and can not be referenced. 
 
By the way, before focussing on details I would like to know if we all 
could 
agree on the following basic structure of a diagram or if we missed 
something fundamental: 
 
- a species can have several graphical representations. 
- a reaction has a place in the diagram (this place could be the center of 
the reaction, the place where the arrows meet) 
- the graphical representation of a speciesreference is a line or curve in 
the diagram leading from a reaction center to a graphical representation 
of 
a species. 
 
So I think that if we want to separate diagram information from the model 
information in the file (which makes sense) there must be a way to refer 
to 
a specific speciesreference. Just now there is no such way in SBML.

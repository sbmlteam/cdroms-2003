On Sat, 24 May 2003, Wagner,John wrote:

> > A membrane IS a compartment of the cell.

> BTW, this isn't necessarily true in SBML

It has to. The word compartment is an abstract term, meaning a portion
of space. A 2D structure is a portion of space (of volume 0).

A membrane is a physical structure, wich separate two other
compartments. BTW a membrane is not necessarily identical to a 2D
compartment. It can be necessary to model it as two adjacent 2D
compartments, or even a 3D compartment.

> this clarified in Florida. Beyond the obvious three-dimensional
> nature of the "volume" attribute, there is a question of the
> "units" attribute, in particular, the units of the units
> assigned to it. That is, is it legal to set "units" equal
> to a set of units that are not volumetric? Could I have:
> 
> <compartment name="PlasmaMembrane" volume="12" units="micronsSquared" />
> 
> (assuming I had already defined "micronsSquared"), or even,
> 
> <compartment name="PlasmaMembrane" volume="12" units="becquerel" />

The problem here is not the element "compartment" but the attribute
"volume". This is why I like your proposal "Membranes", which I would
rename "extendedCompartment" of something like that.

hsauro> Ben, how do you distinguish between a modifier and a reactant?

Hi Herbert,

     The function follows the current SBML L2 specification as written.
It parses the KineticLaw, extracts all symbols and for each symbol,
adds it to the current Reaction as a ModifierSpeciesReference iff:

       1. The symbol is defined as a Species in the current Model, and
       2. The symbol is not a Reactant or Product in the current Reaction.

     In light of recent discussions on this topic, this may be naive or
inappropriate in the larger scheme of things, but without any additional
information or constraints its an effective helper function for converting
L1 models (which do not have the notion of modifiers) to L2 or simply
adding modifiers to your reactions to be compliant with the current
specification.

     Does this make sense?


Ben

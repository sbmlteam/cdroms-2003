On Thu, 22 May 2003, Wagner,John wrote:

> First, we HAVE TO HAVE membranes. 

> What we really want is a completely separate
> element, called membrane; this element will
> then serve as a container for all of the
> *other* membrane pieces we want/need

I strongly disagree with that statement. We HAVE TO HAVE compartments of
various dimensionality. The example you give just require a 2-D
compartment, sandwiched between 3D-compartments. If we begin to create
SBML elements representing biological components, we'll end up having a
mapping between SBML and the component vocabulary of Gene Ontology.

In the 1D, you'll have <microfilament>, <microtubule>, then in
addition to the <membrane>, you'll need things like <lipid_rafts> etc.

<digression>
There is something to dig here. It would be potentially very useful to
have an attribute "goid" attached to compartments and species.
<digression>

And we're just talking about the cell. But Systems Biology is about
global study of biological systems, whatever the organizational
level. Let's think to the Noble/Hunter simulations of whole organs and
individuals ... 

> <listOfCompartments>
> 	<compartment name="Extracellular" volume="1" ...>
> 	<membrane name="PlasmaMembrane"  area="17" outside="Extracellular" ...>
> 	<compartment name="Cytosol"  volume="1" outside="PlasmaMembrane" ...>
> </listOfCompartments>

Why not really expand the compartment element to describe it? For
instance (it is not a proposal, do not comment the specific elements)

<listOfCompartments>
	 <compartment name="Cytosol" dimension="3" volume="4e-12" />
	 <compartment name="DendriteMembrane" dimension="2.5" length1="10" units="µm" />
	 <compartment name="RaftSurface" dimension="2" length1="50" length2="200" units="nm" />
	 <compartment name="VesicleMembrane" dimension="2" surface="1e-14" units="sqnm" />
	 <compartment name="Convoluted_compartment">
		      <geometry>
				<math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">
                                ...
				</math>
		      </geometry>	      
         <compartment>
</listOfCompartments

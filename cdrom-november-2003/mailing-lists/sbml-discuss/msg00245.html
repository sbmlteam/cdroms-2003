I am not terribly enthusiastic about SVG in this context (I used it
before and am familliar with the tools/API that you can use to generate
and parse it).

My lack of enthusiasm is mostly because I do not think that full rendering
details are needed in the SBML file. I see SBML as a way to exchange
models among modeling environments.

To take an analogy with the tools of structural biology, SBML is the PDB
file (a lot better, of course).

People interested in making neat snapshot of a molecular structure, for
publication or other use, read the PDB file with a structure viewer
(Quanta, Insight, RasMol, VMD, etc).

They tune the 3D rendering to show what they need, etc. The rendering
is not written back to the PDB file.

I expect similar viewers to be developed on top of SBML. Each one
with its own strength and weaknesses.

Some diagram viewers that import SBML may support exporting the
diagram to SVG or other high-quality format.

If you need to keep references from an SBML model to high-quality
rendering, better in my opinion to use URLs to allow SBML to support
various graphics formats:

<diagram-renderings>
 <diagram href="http://server1.edu/sb/models/rendering1.gif"/>
 <diagram href="http://server1.edu/sb/models/rendering1.svg"/>
 <diagram href="http://server1.edu/sb/models/rendering1.pdf"/>
</diagram-renderings>

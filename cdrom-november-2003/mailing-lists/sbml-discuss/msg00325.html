I agree (and have always stated this) that the overall reaction rate 
should be in substance/time. The only alternative would be for a 
reaction to be associated to a compartment, because then the volume of 
the compartment where the reaction takes place ("cell" in the above 
formula) could be inferred. For transport processes, explicit volumes of 
the two compartments involved) would still appear in the formula.

> 
>>formula="cell * (Vm * S0)/(Km + S0) "
> 
> the only way to allow such a formulation is if
> Vm is in terms of total volume (the sum of the
> volumes of all compartments), which is obviously
> why you had to multiply by "cell".
> 
> And that is to say that, while in your approach
> you don't have to change initial conditions, you
> instead have to change reaction rates (like Vm
> in your example).
> 
I am afraid, I am not quite with you here.

> 
>>I disagree with Nicholas who wants to remove the
>>two different units used for the species id by
>>making a species id in a rate law represent
>>amout, not concentration.
> 
> I'm with Nicolas on this one, unless someone can
> see a way around it. 

What follows is not an argument about the units of substance ID in rate 
laws:

 > All I know is, it's far
> superior if the overall units of rate laws are
> substance/time, since that always works independent
> of which compartments species are in.

We all agree with that, and that is also why the current and
future standard says that these are the units of the rate law.

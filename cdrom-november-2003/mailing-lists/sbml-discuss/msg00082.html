I am working on a tool to handle SBML files in a piece of software related to
E-cell 1. In this process I am running into the problem that SBML level 1 
seems to use only molecule quantities, not concentrations:

Standard units for substance are mole (I assume this means 6.02 E23 * item,
the document is not clear on this),
possible other definitions are item (molecule count) and mol/liter (the M used 
in chemistry and biology). One problem is that I am not sure which software 
actually supports the use of units, and of converting between quantity and 
concentration, taking into account the volume. I saw one comment in 
JDesigner, which stated that all units are assumed to be compatible, this 
implies that volume is not changing and only molecule counts are used.  When 
both Michealis-menten type laws and mass action laws are used, careful 
attention need to be paid to the question of which units are used for the 
parameters.

Also, it is not clear to me what a use of "volume" as the unit in 
<specie initialamout="0.1" unit="volume"> 
would mean (see section 4.4 of sbml-level-1.pdf).

Maybe this issue has been resolved in level2, but from the documents is seems 
that level 2 is supposed to be an extension, not correction.

Thanks for any help on this, Jörg Weimar.

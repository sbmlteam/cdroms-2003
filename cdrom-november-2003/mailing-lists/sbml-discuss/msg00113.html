It's tempting to let the user do anything but in this case I'm not sure.


The trouble is we only currently have stochastic mass action laws, note that
a stochastic rate law has to be dervied for each mechanism, and as far as I
know we only currently have mass action law in stochastic form. I think
Chris Rao has done some work in trying to derive stochastic Michaelis Menten
laws but I don't know how successful he was. I have seen at least one
accepted paper (it was rejected by three referees) which made an inexcusable
error in a stochastic model, they tried to use a hill equation as a
stochastic law with out any justification. I think it's safer to let the
computer derive the rate laws otherwise users are likely to enter all sorts
of laws which violate one or more laws of physics. If we want to allow users
complete freedom in their choice of rate laws then the simulator must be
able to identify any rough rate laws.

Herbert

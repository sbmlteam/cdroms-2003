I glanced over the "Support for Membrane" discussion and found out
that we have a long way ahead of us to enable evolutionary changes to
SBML to allow spatial modeling. I don't think we have much chance to
bring truly multi-dimensional language constructs soon.
I like the idea to incorporate 0D, 1D, 2D, 2.5D, 3D, etc. compartments
with spatial dimension scales (length, area, volume, mass). They need
to be function of time and should be considered as dependent variables
(if constant we will use initial conditions).

I am not sure if any bio cell ODE solver has tried to incorporate spatial
diffusive advective fluxes (it can be easily done) and time varying integral
formulation but we have to assume that before full blown 3D cell models
are linked to SBML we should plan to accommodate "spatial fluxes" in ODEs.
We will needed to make sure that 1D objects (filaments, axons) have length
that can be part of the solution process e.g. F actin polymerization form
alpha-actin.
How to model supercoiled DNA (length + size or just volume) is another
question?
Similarly membrane should have independent surface area and size
(e.g. mitochondrial membrane can be very convoluted) and again membrane
surface area should be a dependent variable.

The other issue I would like to raise is the consistency of volumes,
characteristic dimensions, and the inheritance of internally embedded
volumes (e.g. nucleus in cytoplasm, or virus translocating in a cytoplasm).
I will need to calculate the mass of a volume compartment with and
without the sub-compartments. Again the volume or mass of the
compartment should be the dependent variable.

I do not know yet enough about SBML to propose the solutions
but we have significant experience in solving PDEs for cellular
biology to help at lease as a sanity test. During the next couple
of yeas we will work with LBL on spatial models and SBML
will be an issue for us as well.

On Thu, 29 May 2003, Michael Hucka wrote:

> I'm not sure if I understand what you mean, but it *sounds*
> like option C in the survey question on membranes.  The
> notion in that option was to have a superclass of
> compartment and membrane, with subclasses for compartment,
> membrane and possibly other things in the future.

No. I meant what John and myself have exemplified last week, that is to
keep the listOfCompartments, but to "enhance" the capability of the
element Compartment, as you described in the option B or what John put
in his "membrane" proposal. Again, I think to define a Membrane beside
the Compartment is really a bad bad idea:

- Membrane and Compartment are two words which belong to different
  vocabularies, and are thus not to be put in parallel. A membrane is a
  physical structure. A compartment is an operational concept. To add a
  Membrane beside Compartment is like to add Acid or Aldehyde beside
  Species.
- A membrane of the cell IS a compartment.
- There are other 2D compartments beside membranes.
- There are other structures beside cytosol and membrane. To define a
  Membrane element is a very slipery process. 

If a Compartment is flexible enough to encode 2D objects, people will be
able to implement biological membranes as 2D surfaces, sandwiches of 2D
surfaces, or volumes, as they wich.

Hello All,

Though some of the points Jörg and Herbert are making are correct. It is not SBML's task to decide which models are valid and what methods to use to establish trajectories. 

Thats why we have no information regarding what to do with the model within SBML! If you decide to restrict stochastic models to Mass Action Kinetics for once and for all SBML might be the place to do that. But I strongly believe that is not the case. 

In my opinion it is the tools and the scientist duty to investigate which approach is meaningful for a certain model and not SBML's. You will use SBML to transport the model between the tools and compare the results.

I hope that makes my point more clear. We can discuss the issue in more detail in Fort Lauderdale.

Stefan

> Dear All,
> 
> I strongly agree with Herbert Sauro that for stochastic simulations, not 
> all rate laws are consistent. But the rate is not the only quantity of 
> interest. In stochastic simulations in their simplest form (Gillespie 
> method), we specify the rate and implicitly specify that the 
> distribution of waiting times between reactive events is a Poisson 
> distribution. But other distributions are possible, e.g. Gamma 
> distributions for events that are modeled as one step, but really 
> consist of a number of consequtive steps (Translation and Transkription 
> are prominent examples). For other distributions, not only the rate, but 
> the shape or name of the distribution and other parameters must be 
> specified.
> 
> Such information can currently only be specified in the notes. Related 
> deterministic models use time-delay differential equations, which are 
> difficult to solve (and even to establish the existence of solutions), 
> and as far as I can see, impossible to specify in SBML.
> 
> But then, SBML is evolving, and the first version is a good starting 
> point. Unfortunately, I will not be able to come to Florida to furher 
> discuss things in person, so please publish notes.
> 
> Jörg Weimar.

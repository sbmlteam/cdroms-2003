Sven

IMHO we should be not too worried about the
L2 annotated form of the diagram proposal.
If we can get agreement on the diagram form
over the summer we might be able to get it
into SBML itself before the end of the year
given community agreement either as L2 version 2
or Level 3 version 1.  IMHO we should focus
on developing a minimal graphics proposal
that is obviously and easily extensible.
It is natural that the diagram stuff is
the next thing that should go into the standard
as diagrams are widely supported by existing software.

I'm not convinced (or at least need more convincing)
that its a good idea
to allow diagraming of reactions and structures
that are not formally part of the main model.
Doing this really implies that there are ways
of modelling the same system which perhaps
should be captured in another SBML model.
In addition if this extra information is captured
only in the diagram part of the model it becomes
inaccessable to other tools that could make use
of it.  I have no problem with unstructured
pictures as part of the diagram but I think
we should try to get as much biological information
into the formal model and out of the diagrams.

With respect to the L2 annotation:
IMHO I think having an id field annotation on speciesReference
and not having a refType field on the speciesReferenceGraphic
element is the best approach:

a) it leads us to the approach we're most likley to adopt
in L3

b) it allows us to support A+A->B reactions
